# Slashing and Economic Security

Slashing is the primary economic defense mechanism that aligns node operator incentives with the security of the network.

## What is Slashing?

When a node misbehaves, the protocol can confiscate (slash) a portion of its bonded RUNE. This is the "skin in the game" that makes attacking the network economically irrational.

## Types of Slashing Events

- **Observation failures**: Failing to report inbound or outbound transactions.
- **Signing failures**: Not participating in TSS signing when required.
- **Double signing or equivocation**: Attempting to produce conflicting blocks or signatures.

## Slash Rate

The current slash rate is **1.5x** the value of the harm caused. This over-slashing makes attacks extremely expensive relative to any potential gain.

## Churn and Unbonding

Nodes that accumulate too many slash points can be forced out of the active set during churn. Even without forced churn, nodes have a 12-hour unbonding period after leaving the active set, during which their bond is still at risk.

## Why This Matters

Without strong slashing, a rational actor could attempt to steal funds or disrupt the network if the expected value of the attack exceeded the bond at risk. The combination of high bonds (~300k–2M+ RUNE), 1.5x slash multiplier, and regular churning creates one of the strongest economic security models in decentralized finance.

The system has been tested through multiple real-world incidents and has continued to evolve its monitoring and response mechanisms.
